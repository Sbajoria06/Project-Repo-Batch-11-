At the time Mary had noted nothing of these things, now she saw them all. As for the first time, in minute detail, while slowly she went up the stair and threw the narrowed ways, and heard the same wind that raved alike about the new grave and the old house, into which latter, for all the bails banked against the walls, it found many a chain of entrance. The last strains of the ill-treated, ill-fated intermezzo had died away, and after them had died away also the rumbling of the wheels of the murderous barrel organ that had so galley executed that, along with the nine other tunes of its repatory, to the admiration of the housemaid at the window of the house opposite, and the crowing delight of the two babies next door. This violent and triumphant revolution in his prospects and his fortunes was hardly yet completely comprehended by our friend Ferdinand Armin, and when he had left a note for the generous Mirobel, whose slumbers he would not disturb at this early hour, even with good news, he strolled along Up Charles Street and to the park, in one of those wild and joyous reveries in which we brood over coming bliss and create a thousand glorious consequences. In exactly ten minutes it is in the power of every man to free himself from all the tumult of the world, the pangs of love, the throbs of ambition, the wear and tear of play, the recriminating buddhwar, the conspiring club, the rattling hell, and find himself in a sublime Sylvan solitude superior to the cedars of Lebanon, and inferior only in extent to the chestnut forest of Anatolia. Often would he avail himself of any special grace of his own idiom and epoch if only his will belonged to him. Often would some cadence serve him, but for his author's structure, some structure but for his author's cadence. Often the beautiful turn of a stanza must be weakened to adopt some rhyme which will tally, and he sees the poet reveling in abundance of language where himself is scantily supplied.
